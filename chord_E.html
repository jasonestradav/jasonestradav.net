Chord E

The easy way of playing the Chord E consists of 3 fingers

The chord is played with 3 fingers, the circles represent the position of the fingers

Starting from the bottom to the top

Your index finger will be placed where the circle is located
If you look at the image above to the right the EADGBE
displayed is the notes when tuned
The note that is played is G#/Ab
either G# or Ab is correct

Your middle finger will be placed where the circle is located
If you look at the image above to the right the EADGBE
displayed is the notes when tuned
The note that is played is B

Your ring finger will be placed where the circle is located
If you look at the image above to the right the EADGBE
displayed is the notes when tuned
The note that is played is E

When the chord is placed together, this is what it will look like from different views

When you strum the guitar, your guitar pick/finger needs to
play the Low top E string to the bottom High E string so
you will be strumming all 6 strings
X = Do Not Play String
O = Play String
